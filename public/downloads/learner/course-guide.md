# Course Guide

## Course
Modern AI in Practice: Foundations, Codex, and OpenClaw

## Audience
- Primary audience: executives, technical managers, product leaders, engineering leads, and innovation leads responsible for AI adoption decisions.
- Secondary audience: senior practitioners who need a business-operating view rather than a deep implementation workshop.

## Prerequisites
- No coding is required.
- Participants should understand how their teams currently deliver work, approve changes, and manage operational risk.
- Participants should come with one business or operational workflow in mind for the capstone blueprint.

## Delivery mode
- 2 days
- 8 contact hours total
- Demo-led, discussion-heavy, executive-friendly
- Approximate ratio: 30% concepts, 40% demos and guided walkthroughs, 30% structured exercises

## What this course is
- A practical foundations course on modern AI systems, coding agents, orchestration, and safe pilot design.
- A decision-making course for leaders who need to evaluate where AI can create value without introducing unmanaged risk.

## What this course is not
- Not a deep technical build workshop for engineers.
- Not a survey of every AI model, provider, or research trend.
- Not a deep dive into training, fine-tuning, or advanced MLOps.

## Measurable outcomes
By the end of the course, participants should be able to:
- classify an AI use case as chatbot, retrieval workflow, automation, or agentic workflow
- explain the difference between model capability, context, tools, memory, and orchestration
- describe the Codex workflow of inspect, plan, implement, validate, review, and summarize
- identify where AGENTS.md, MCP, skills, approvals, and sandboxing fit in Codex practice
- explain where OpenClaw fits in a multi-channel agent architecture
- complete a pilot blueprint with users, controls, success metrics, and a 30-60-90 day next step

## Participant outputs
- One completed exercise workbook
- One completed pilot blueprint
- One practical view of where Codex and OpenClaw fit, and where they do not

## Governance thread
Throughout the course, participants will repeatedly answer:
- when should we not automate this workflow
- what data is allowed into the system
- who approves what action
- how will we measure success
- how will we stop or contain the pilot if it fails
