# Facilitator Notes

## Facilitation stance
- Be clear, direct, and practical.
- Use simple language over technical flourish.
- Keep value and governance in the same conversation.

## Audience assumptions
- Mixed leadership room: executives, technical managers, product leaders, and senior operators.
- Some participants may be highly technical; many will not be.
- Do not require technical participation for the course to succeed.

## What success looks like
- Participants can explain the AI system model in business terms.
- Participants can distinguish Codex from generic chat use.
- Participants can explain what OpenClaw adds at the orchestration layer.
- Each team leaves with one realistic pilot blueprint.

## Course-wide teaching reminders
- Name what the course does not cover.
- Repeat the governance thread often.
- Use Codex and OpenClaw as examples of operating models, not as product theatre.
- Bring the discussion back to users, approvals, and measurable outcomes.

## Core governance questions
- When should this not be automated?
- What data is allowed?
- Who approves actions?
- How will success be measured?
- How will the pilot be stopped if it fails?

## Time protection
- Protect demo windows.
- Do not let taxonomy debates consume exercise time.
- If time compresses, shorten discussion before cutting the capstone.

## Fallback mode
- If a live demo fails, switch immediately to screenshots or prepared outputs.
- Preserve the learning objective even if the tooling moment is lost.
