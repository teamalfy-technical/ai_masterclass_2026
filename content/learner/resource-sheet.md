## What To Review After The Course
- Revisit the course guide and your completed pilot blueprint.
- Review the Codex workflow notes on planning, validation, approvals, and project guidance.
- Review the OpenClaw architecture notes on Gateway, sessions, channels, tools, skills, and safe defaults.
- Decide whether your next step is a simple grounded workflow, a coding-agent pilot, or an orchestration pilot.

## Suggested Implementation Artifacts
- One sample task brief with context, constraints, and acceptance criteria.
- One example AGENTS.md file for a pilot repository.
- One validation checklist for AI-assisted work.
- One architecture sketch showing model, retrieval, tools, approvals, and outputs.
- One owner map for product, platform, security, and operations.

## Practical Follow-Up References
- Keep a safe demo repository for Codex testing.
- Pretest approvals, validation commands, and rollback steps before live demonstrations.
- Document which data is allowed, which actions require approval, and how sessions are audited.
- Keep backup screenshots or recordings for every live walkthrough.

## First 30 Days After The Course
- Week 1: choose one bounded workflow and name the sponsor.
- Week 2: draft the task brief, controls, and success metrics.
- Week 3: run a controlled proof of value and review the failure modes.
- Week 4: decide whether to expand, revise, pause, or stop the pilot.
