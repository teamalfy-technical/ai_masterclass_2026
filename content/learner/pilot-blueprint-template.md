# Pilot Blueprint Template

## Instructions
- Use this template to define one practical AI pilot.
- Keep the pilot narrow enough to govern, measurable enough to evaluate, and reversible enough to stop safely.
- Complete every field even if the answer is "unknown today".

## Pilot Name

## Business Owner or Sponsor

## Problem Statement
- What is not working well today?
- Why is this worth solving now?

## Current Workflow
- How is the work handled today?
- What baseline pain points or metrics describe the current state?

## Proposed AI Role
- What specific workflow step will AI help with?
- What will stay human-led?

## Primary Users

## Inputs and Context Sources
- What documents, systems, repositories, or knowledge sources will the workflow rely on?
- What information must never be used?

## Tools, Platforms, and Integrations
- Which tools, agents, or platforms are involved?
- Where do Codex and or OpenClaw fit, if at all?
- Which teams or systems must be included for the pilot to work?

## Data Sensitivity Classification
- Public
- Internal
- Confidential
- Regulated

## Human Review and Approvals
- What actions require approval?
- Who gives that approval?
- What must never happen without a person in the loop?

## Risks and Controls
- What can go wrong?
- What control reduces each risk?
- Who owns each risk?

## Success Metrics
- What two to four measures determine whether the pilot is working?
- What baseline will you compare against?

## Go or No-Go Threshold
- What result would justify continuing, expanding, pausing, or stopping the pilot?

## Rollback or Containment Plan
- If the pilot causes harm or fails to perform, how will you stop, limit, or reverse it?

- Medium
- High

## Adoption and Change Management
- What training, communication, or policy updates are required?
