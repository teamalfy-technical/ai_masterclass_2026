coreIdea:
  - "people often confuse impressive output with real intelligence or autonomy"
  - "an LLM predicts likely next tokens in context — it does not understand the way a human does"
  - "useful AI depends on workflows, tools, validation, and human review — not just a good model"
deliveryLead: >-
  This chapter gives the audience a clear, practical mental model of AI and LLMs before the course moves into prompting, Codex, and orchestration. It opens with a story that challenges assumptions and closes by connecting foundations to practical application.
talkingPoints:
  - AI is a broad umbrella — different systems get called AI for very different reasons
  - machine learning, deep learning, and generative AI are layers, not synonyms
  - LLMs are trained to predict the most likely next token given context
  - fluency is not understanding — a confident answer is not a correct answer
  - hallucinations happen because the model is optimising for plausibility, not truth
  - LLMs excel at summarisation, drafting, classification, extraction, and synthesis
  - LLMs struggle with reasoning, arithmetic, freshness, consistency, and domain specificity
  - useful AI is a system — model plus context, tools, retrieval, validation, and governance
takeaway: >-
  Before asking whether an AI system is intelligent, ask what is actually doing the work — a model, a human, a workflow, hidden labour, or some combination.
